Education

Master's degree in Public Policy from Northwestern University; focus on Education policy.

I'm what Emilie Wapnick refers to as a "multipotentialite." As such, I went through a few different undergrad degree choices before making a decision that stuck.

I started out thinking that I wanted to be a CPA. That lasted until I hit the business statistics course and decided I hated everything about it - if that course was required, they could keep the accounting degree.

Next up was Education. Yep, I was totally going to be a teacher. That one lasted until the semester just before student teaching when I was doing classroom observations. Teaching to tests was the entirety of teaching, it seemed.

Bo-RING!

The foray into education wasn't all bad though; I ended up shifting to political science and focusing all of my research and writing on education policy for the rest of undergrad. This trend carried on into my Master's degree. I wrote my thesis on the impact school-choice programming has on segregated student populations.

I still love education policy, but I'm also really excited to be learning to code through LaunchCode's 101 course. It's a ton of fun, and I really think that this type of work is something I could hang on to for a while.
